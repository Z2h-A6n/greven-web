Collaborations

A welcome byproduct of our crystal growth efforts has been our ability to gain valuable additional insights through extensive collaborations with scientists using complementary experimental techniques:

  • Prof. J. C. Campuzano (U. Illinois and Argonne National Lab) and Prof. J. Fink (Dresden University and IFW, Germany)
    Synchrotron-based dichroic photoemission
  • Prof. I. R. Fisher (Stanford University)
    Uniaxial strain transport
  • Prof. N. Gedik (Massachusetts Institute of Technology)
    Laser-based dichroic photoemission spectroscopy
  • Prof. C. Giannetti (Università Cattolica del Sacro Cuore, Italy)
    Time-resolved reflectivity
  • Prof. J. Haase (Univ. Leipzig)
    NMR
  • Dr. R. Hackl (Walther Meissner Institute, Garching, Germany), Prof. B. Keimer (Max-Planck Institute, Stuttgart, Germany), and Prof. Y. Li (Peking University, China)
    Raman scattering
  • Prof. D. Hsieh (Caltech)
    Laser-based second-harmonic generation experiments
  • Dr. M.-H. Julien (High Magnetic Field Laboratory, Grenoble, France
    NMR
  • Prof. A. Kapitulnik (Stanford University)
    Kerr effect
  • Prof. D. van der Marel (Université de Genève)
    Optical spectroscopy
  • Dr. A. Migliori, Dr. B. Ramshaw, and Dr. A. Shekter (National High Magnetic Field Laboratory)
    Resonant ultrasound
  • Prof. J. Orenstein (UC Berkeley)
    Nonlinear optical measurements
  • Prof. M. Požek (University of Zagreb, Croatia)
    Microwave transport measurements
  • Dr. C. Proust (LNCMI, Toulouse, France)
    High-field magnetoresistivity
  • Prof. R. Prozorov (Iowa State University and Ames National Laboratory)
    Penetration depth measurements
  • Prof. Z.-X. Shen (Stanford University)
    Photoemission spectroscopy
  • Prof. Y. Uemura (Columbia University) and Prof. G. Luke (McMaster University)
    Muon spin relaxation
